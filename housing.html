Ministry of Housing & Urban Development

Managing land monopolies, urban planning grids, and residential allocations. Our housing policy ensures affordable homes for all while maintaining state sovereignty over land.

I. 70-Year Leasehold Model

The State retains absolute ownership of 100% of all land. Citizens may purchase and trade 70-year residential leaseholds on state land. This balances robust private investment and modern urban maintenance while ensuring the government retains ultimate territorial sovereignty. Leaseholds may be renewed upon expiry.

Average residential leasehold prices (2026): Tier-1 city: eK 450k · Tier-2: eK 210k · Rural: eK 75k

II. Hukou Residency System

The Municipal Residency framework manages urban infrastructure capacity. Your residency status (Hukou) determines your immediate access to local medical clinics, schooling, and certain social benefits. Transfers to Tier-1 megacities are algorithmically managed by the Ministry of Home Affairs to prevent urban over-saturation.

III. Social Housing & Rent Control

The State provides subsidised rental housing for low-income workers, essential public servants, and priority industries. Rent is capped at 15% of household income. Approximately 34% of urban households live in state-provided social housing.
