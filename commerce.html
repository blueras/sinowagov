Ministry of Commerce & Technological Innovation

Regulating the private sector, managing Special Economic Zones (SEZs), and driving industrial growth under state guidance. Our economy combines dynamic private enterprise with strategic state oversight.

State-Guided Capitalism & Corporate Oversight

Sinowa supports a vibrant private sector to drive innovation and export growth. However, under our State-Guided framework, the State Council retains a "Golden Share" (1%) and a mandatory board seat in all major corporations. This ensures private profit generation never diverges from the collective national interest.

Key Sectors: Advanced manufacturing · Green energy · Semiconductor design · Pharmaceutical research · Digital infrastructure

Special Economic Zones (SEZs)

Foreign investors may operate within designated SEZs with streamlined regulations, tax incentives, and reduced red tape. All SEZ transactions are monitored by the Central Bank to prevent capital flight. Current SEZs: Pearl River Delta Tech Hub (Tier 1), Northern Industrial Corridor (Tier 2), Western Development Zone (emerging).

Foreign Investment Highlights 2025-26: eK 2.3B committed · 47 new factories · 12,000 jobs created

Domestic Trade & Supply Chains

The State manages strategic reserves and critical supply chains (grain, energy, rare earths, pharmaceuticals). Private logistics companies operate under licence. The SinowaLink app integrates all commercial payments, inventory tracking, and vendor certification.
