Social Credit System

Following State Council Directive 915, civic behaviours translate directly into an algorithmic scoring matrix. Positive contributions including exceeding industrial targets and completing volunteer shifts improve scores. Infractions yield consequences including restricted transit and lower service priorities.

Universal Public Health System

Universal healthcare funded by the State. All citizens and permanent residents receive full coverage. Digital health records integrate with SinowaLink.

Health Statistics: Life expectancy: 79.2 years · Infant mortality: 3.2 per 1,000 · Average wait time for specialist: 14 days · Hospitals: 247 nationwide

Integrated Transport Planning and High-Speed Rail

National ID cards function as contactless transit tokens across all bus networks and metro systems. High-Speed Rail Phase I with 3 lines spanning 1,200 kilometres is operational. Phase II engineering is underway with expected completion in 2028 adding 800 kilometres. Integrated ticketing via SinowaLink.

State Land Monopoly and Municipal Allocation

The State retains exclusive ownership over 100 per cent of the land. Private land ownership, real-estate speculation, and bourgeois rent-extraction are permanently prohibited. Residential quarters and manufacturing sites are allocated strictly on a collective utility framework baseline.

Public Welfare and Pensions

State pension for all citizens aged 65 and over averages eK 1,200 per month. Unemployment benefit is 60 per cent of previous salary for up to 12 months. Child benefit is eK 300 per month per child up to age 18. Disability support includes comprehensive coverage including home care.
