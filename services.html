II. Social Credit System

Following State Council Directive 915, civic behaviours translate directly into an algorithmic scoring matrix. Positive contributions (exceeding industrial targets, completing volunteer shifts) improve scores. Infractions yield consequences including restricted transit and lower service priorities.

III. Universal Public Health System

Universal healthcare funded by the State. All citizens and permanent residents receive full coverage. Digital health records integrate with SinowaLink.

Health Statistics: Life expectancy: 79.2 years · Infant mortality: 3.2/1,000 · Average wait time for specialist: 14 days · Hospitals: 247 nationwide

IV. Integrated Transport Planning & High-Speed Rail

National ID cards function as contactless transit tokens across all bus networks and metro systems. HSR Phase I (3 lines, 1,200 km) operational; Phase II engineering underway (expected completion 2028, +800 km). Integrated ticketing via SinowaLink.

V. State Land Monopoly & Municipal Allocation

The State retains exclusive ownership over 100% of the land. Private land ownership, real-estate speculation, and bourgeois rent-extraction are permanently prohibited. Residential quarters and manufacturing sites are allocated strictly on a collective utility framework baseline.

VI. Public Welfare & Pensions

State pension for all citizens aged 65+ (average: eK 1,200/month). Unemployment benefit: 60% of previous salary for up to 12 months. Child benefit: eK 300/month per child (up to age 18). Disability support: comprehensive coverage including home care.
