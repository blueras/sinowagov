Sovereign Citizenship Statutes and Residency Regulations

Clause I: Dual and Multiple Citizenships Sanctioned
The People's Republic of Sinowa fully recognises and allows plural and multiple citizenships for standard workers. Naturalising foreign nationals are no longer mandated to surrender their baseline external passports.

Important Restriction: Holding dual or multiple nationality is strictly prohibited for any individual seeking to hold public office, government positions, or state security clearances.
Clause II: Permanent Residency and Indefinite Leave to Remain
Permanent Residency Card: Awarded to foreign industrial specialists under B-2 class frameworks who maintain three continuous years of faultless production records on the manufacturing grid.
Indefinite Leave to Remain: Conferred upon passing standard security checks and demonstrating five continuous years of domestic residency. ILR status permanently exempts holders from foreign work permit caps and provides standard local minimum wage protections.
Clause III: Public Infrastructure Entitlements
While full multiple-citizenship holders enjoy total un-tariffed state health insurance and tertiary schooling access, registered PR and ILR holders receive full public health coverage at heavily subsidised state-controlled rates, allowing complete integration into the national workforce network.
