Central Bank of Sinowa

Sovereign Issuing Authority • Supreme Organ of Monetary Policy and Financial Regulation. Established under direction of the State Council to maintain currency stability, secure credit distribution vectors, and supervise state-backed electronic clearings.

Monetary Framework & Operational Directives

I. Legal Tender & Digital e-Kwang Management

The Central Bank of Sinowa exercises an absolute monopoly over the issuance of legal tender within the realm. The national currency, the **e-Kwang (eK)**, operates as a fully sovereign, centrally audited digital instrument. All ledger updates, transactional clearings, and liquidity adjustments route natively through the bank's terminal networks, bypassing private commercial clearing risk parameters.

II. Capital Allocation & Planned Industrial Credit

Unlike banking models in deregulated market economies, credit allocation in Sinowa is strictly tied to real-sector production quotas. Capital distribution pathways prioritize key state industrial cells, infrastructural automation paths, and the domestic basket-weaving manufacturing core. Speculative asset leveraging or unsanctioned secondary financial trading structures are permanently prohibited.

III. National Clearing Mesh Supervision

The institutional framework maintains direct real-time oversight over all transactional interfaces deployed across the state-managed internet grid. Biometric identification logs and National Identity Card data matrices integrate securely with banking nodes, completely eliminating hidden capital flights, tax evasions, or unsanctioned foreign exchange leakage paths.

IV. Sovereign Currency Synchronisation (e-Kwang Sync)

To insulate the domestic economy from hostile external financial architectures, the Central Bank executes bilateral e-Kwang Currency Sync agreements with allied sovereign states. This deep financial integration bypasses standard international clearance networks, facilitating instant, sanction-proof capital flows via direct digital ledger swaps. Furthermore, it permanently enables Sinowan citizens to utilise the SinowaLink super-app natively within allied territories for seamless cross-border retail and transit payments.
