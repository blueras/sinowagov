Sovereign Citizenship Statutes & Residency Regulations

Clause I: Dual & Multiple Citizenships Sanctioned

Following updated legislative adjustments by the State Council, the People's Republic of Sinowa **fully recognises and allows plural and multiple citizenships**. Naturalising foreign nationals are no longer mandated to surrender their baseline external passports, and native Sinowan citizens may legally acquire external documentation without losing their domestic Tier-1 socialist infrastructure rights.

Clause II: Permanent Residency (PR) & Indefinite Leave to Remain (ILR)

For foreign nationals who do not immediately seek full multi-citizenship, the state provides a secure long-term settlement track administered through specialised status tiers:

  • Permanent Residency (PR) Card: Awarded to foreign industrial specialists under B-2 class frameworks who maintain three continuous years of faultless production records on the manufacturing grid.
  • Indefinite Leave to Remain (ILR): Conferred upon passing standard security checks and demonstrating five continuous years of domestic residency. ILR status permanently exempts holders from foreign work permit caps and provides standard local minimum wage protections.

Clause III: Public Infrastructure Entitlements

While full multiple-citizenship holders enjoy total un-tariffed state health insurance and tertiary schooling access, registered PR and ILR holders receive full public health coverage at heavily subsidised state-controlled rates, allowing complete integration into the national workforce network.
